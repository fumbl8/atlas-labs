# Biodiversity Signal Triage Notebook

**Public occurrence data into transparent attention candidates**

This notebook-style workflow explores how biodiversity observation records can be turned into site-level triage signals: observed richness, watchlist signals, pressure indicators, sparse-taxon hits, data gaps, and follow-up attention candidates.

## What it demonstrates

- Public biodiversity-data workflow design.
- Provenance and source-summary thinking.
- Ecological triage feature engineering.
- Data-gap and sampling-bias awareness.
- Clear separation between attention queues and decisions.

## Public-facing boundary

The output should be read as a transparent attention queue, not as a validated conservation priority, species distribution model, survey result, or management recommendation.
