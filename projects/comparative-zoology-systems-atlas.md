# Comparative Zoology Systems Atlas

**Animal adaptations as mechanisms, ecological roles, and biomimicry signals**

This comparative atlas treats animals as living systems: sensing, movement, sociality, communication, environmental engineering, ecological function, and design lessons.

## What it demonstrates

- Organismal-biology curiosity.
- Comparative adaptation framing.
- Zoology and science communication.
- Biomimicry without flattening the animal into a gadget metaphor.

## Public-facing boundary

This is an interpretive v0 card system, not a field guide, taxonomic reference, or expert-reviewed zoology database. Trait scores are comparison tags, not quantitative measurements.
