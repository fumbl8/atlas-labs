# Biological Materials Inspiration Atlas

**Organism strategies translated into design principles and validation cautions**

This atlas organizes biological structures — such as nacre, gecko adhesion, mussel byssus, lotus surfaces, shark skin, spider silk, bone, plant actuation, insect wings, and desert water-collection analogs — into design principles, material strategies, application concepts, and caution notes.

## What it demonstrates

- Biomimicry and materials-translation framing.
- Structured science communication.
- Design-principle extraction.
- Pairing inspiration with testing and validation cautions.

## Public-facing boundary

This is an inspiration and concept-translation artifact, not a validated materials database or engineering recommendation set. Cards need formal citations, literature-backed notes, feasibility scoring, and target-use testing before technical claims.
