## Highlight: BioSignals

### BioSignals: Disease Mechanism Cartographer

**Tagline:** Mapping fragmented biomedical evidence into auditable disease mechanism maps.

BioSignals is the most directly relevant life-sciences hackathon concept in this public portfolio. It explores how Claude can help extract biological signals, proposed causal relationships, evidence strength, contradictions, convergence points, and unresolved research questions from biomedical literature into inspectable mechanism maps.

- **Domain:** ALS / neurodegeneration as an initial demo area.
- **Core artifacts:** signal map, evidence cards, convergence view, contradiction ledger, research next-step generator, plain-language translation.
- **Public repo:** [fumbl8/atlas-biosignals](https://github.com/fumbl8/atlas-biosignals)
- **Boundary:** research-navigation prototype, not a diagnostic or clinical decision tool.

## How to read this portfolio

This is not meant to imply that every project is equally mature. The portfolio is best read as a pattern of work:

1. Pick a complex living system.
2. Build a small model, map, notebook, or index around it.
3. Make the output visible and explainable.
4. State what the artifact can and cannot support.
5. Identify the next validation step.

The strongest thread is not any one finished app. It is the habit of converting biological and ecological complexity into structured, inspectable, uncertainty-aware artifacts.
